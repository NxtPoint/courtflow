# Social Media Loop — the standing discipline

> Social isn't a burst of posts then silence — it's a loop, the same as paid: **Plan → Post → Measure →
> Learn → Repeat.** The whole point of this doc is that social **runs itself** instead of stalling.
> Sister doc to `optimization-loop.md` (paid/SEO). Owner: Tomo + Cowork (strategy/captions) / Tomo
> (shooting + publishing via Meta Business Suite).

## The one rule that governs everything
**Optimise toward the free first lesson, not the like.**
Followers and views are *diagnostics*, never the goal. The metric closest to money is **free-lesson
bookings from social** (and the DMs/profile-link taps that precede them). A post with 200 views that
sends 3 people to book beats a 5,000-view reel that sends none. Same discipline as paid: bottom of the
funnel wins. *(Once CourtFlow is live, the free-lesson booking becomes a tracked conversion — at which
point social ties into the same `core.usage_event` stream as everything else.)*

## Why we can't quit
Social rewards **consistency over polish**. The competitor audit is blunt about it: the whole JHB tennis
field is quiet and lookalike (`social-competitor-audit.md`). The club that simply *shows up every week*
wins the category. A dark week costs more than a mediocre post. So the loop's job is to make "never go
dark" the path of least resistance.

## The loop
1. **Plan** — pick the week's posts from `social-content-batch.md` (or the next batch). The calendar is
   pre-built; planning is *selection*, not invention.
2. **Post** — schedule the week ahead in **Meta Business Suite** (FB + IG at once) in one sitting. Batch =
   no daily decisions. Stories are the daily low-effort layer (polls, reposts, schedule cards).
3. **Measure** — pull the numbers (see metrics below). Insights live free inside the IG Professional
   account + Meta Business Suite.
4. **Learn** — which *format/pillar* drove profile taps + free-lesson clicks? Reels usually win reach;
   offers win action. Note the 2–3 best and worst.
5. **Repeat** — make *more of what worked*, cut what didn't, refill footage before the well runs dry.
   Each week is a marginal gain that compounds.

## The metrics that matter (in priority order)
1. **Free-lesson clicks / DMs / bookings from social** — the money metric. Track link taps + "book"
   enquiries that mention Instagram. *(Becomes a hard conversion once CourtFlow tracking is live.)*
2. **Profile visits & link taps** — intent signal; the step before booking.
3. **Saves & shares** — the real reach driver on IG (worth more than likes; they signal value).
4. **Reach / new followers** — top-of-funnel diagnostic. Growth is good, but only as a *means* to #1.
5. **Likes/comments** — lowest priority; engagement hygiene, not a goal. (Do reply to all of them — the
   algorithm rewards it and it's warm-lead care.)

## Decision triggers (let the data pick the direction)
- **Good reach, no profile taps / clicks?** Top-of-funnel works, the *ask* is weak. Strengthen the CTA and
  post more offer/programme content (Pillar 4).
- **Good clicks, no bookings?** It's a *funnel/landing-page* problem, not a content problem — fix the
  free-lesson page, exactly as in the paid loop. Don't blame the reel.
- **One pillar/format outperforms (e.g. clay reels, coach tips)?** Make more of it. Double down on winners,
  quietly retire losers.
- **Engagement flat for weeks?** Usually a *consistency* or *hook* problem — check you didn't go dark, and
  that line 1 of recent posts actually stops the scroll.
- **Thinking about paid (Meta Ads)?** Only once organic is consistently producing engaged local followers —
  then retarget them cheaply. Warm > cold. (See `channels-and-tools.md` §2.)
