### What I CAN do with video (no filming)
- **Cut & sequence** your existing clips into reels with branded intro/outro cards.
- **Burn in captions, hooks and CTAs**, brand colours, your handle.
- **Reframe** landscape footage to vertical (crop or on-brand letterbox).
- **Ken Burns motion** — turn your *photos* into moving clips, so even still-only weeks get a reel.
- Trim, speed-ramp, stitch, add fades.

### What I CANNOT do
- **Film or generate new footage.** I work with what exists. I also don't add licensed music — you add
  **trending audio inside Instagram** when you post (it's free and actually boosts reach).

### How we get *great* reels (the unlock)
The ceiling on video quality = the footage. The launch clips are phone-shot and a bit shaky/fenced, so
this first reel is good, not cinematic. To make genuinely scroll-stopping reels, the media guy should grab
(vertical, 9:16, phone is fine):
- **Clay-court slides & rallies** — close, clean, no fence in front.
- **Slow-mo serves** (side + behind).
- **Coach tips to camera** (Coach Ross) — these make the best reels of all.
- **A "day at NextPoint"** set of 5–10 short b-roll clips (gates, courts, golden hour).

Give me 10–15 good clips and I'll turn them into a month of reels. (Full spec is in
`social-media-brief-for-videographer.md`.)
