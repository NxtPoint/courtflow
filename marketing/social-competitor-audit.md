# Social Competitor & Benchmark Audit — JHB Tennis (June 2026)

> Honest read of the field we're posting into, who's actually winning attention, and the gaps NextPoint
> can own. Owner: Tomo + Cowork. Pairs with `social-media-strategy.md` (what to post) and
> `social-loop.md` (the cadence that keeps it going).

## How this was done (and its limits)
Ahrefs' social module isn't on our current plan, and it only tracks channels *we* connect anyway — it
can't see competitors. So this is built from **public web + profile research** (search, the
[Joburg ETC 2026 academy ranking](https://www.joburgetc.com/things-to-do/outdoor-parks/top-tennis-coaching-academies-johannesburg-2026/),
and competitor Instagram/Facebook pages). Treat follower numbers as **indicative, not audited** —
Instagram gates exact counts and post history behind login, so cadence reads are directional. The
*strategic* conclusions below hold regardless of a few hundred followers either way.

## 2. What the audit actually found

**a) The social bar is LOW. This is the headline.**
Nobody in this field is a social powerhouse. The strongest accounts we found sit in the **hundreds to
~1,600 followers** (e.g. an Evolve-branded account ~1.6k; most others a few hundred), and several rely on
**Facebook over Instagram** or post sporadically. There is **no dominant local tennis voice on Instagram.**
That means the "we're terrible at this" feeling is relative to *nobody* — a consistent, well-produced feed
could become the #1 JHB tennis account inside ~6 months. The opportunity is winning by *showing up*.

**b) "Free first lesson" is NOT a unique hook.**
TNT and Next Generation also lead with a free/first lesson. We've been treating it as our differentiator —
it's table stakes. Keep it as the *CTA*, but it can't be the *story*.

**c) Most competitors sell the coaching; almost none sell a place.**
The field is overwhelmingly "service" accounts (lessons, squads, timetables, results). Very few have a
**signature venue or visual identity** to anchor a feed. They look interchangeable. This is the gap.

**d) Two demand currents are running hot in 2026:**
- **Early junior development** — the ranking calls out a citywide shift into very young age groups; parents
  in WhatsApp groups reward *patient coaches* + *programmes kids want to return to*.
- **Padel pressure** — Joburg ETC is publishing padel content alongside tennis; padel is pulling casual
  racquet players. Tennis clubs that tell a *why tennis* story defend their turf.

## 3. NextPoint's unfair advantages (what nobody else can copy)
Lining our assets against the field, three are genuinely defensible:

1. **The only clay court in Gauteng.** No competitor in the list has this. It's a one-of-a-kind visual *and*
   a development story (movement, point construction). This is our single most ownable piece of content — and
   it's being under-used.
2. **Experienced, tour-level coaching** — led by Head Coach Ross Nemeth, with a coaching team whose
   background includes working with tour-level and high-performance players. Competitors mostly market the
   *academy*; we can market genuine coaching depth and results.
3. **A real venue with scale + setting** — 8 courts at Killarney, Houghton. A place worth filming. Most rivals
   coach on borrowed school/club courts with no visual signature.

> Strategic read: the field competes on *coaching claims* (everyone says "structured, all levels, juniors").
> We should compete on **place + people + the clay** — things that are true only of us and that *show*
> beautifully on Instagram. Sell the experience; let the free lesson be the door.

## 5. Benchmark targets (so the loop has a scoreboard)
Because the field is small, set **beat-the-field** targets, not vanity ones:
- **90 days:** out-post the whole field on consistency (≥3 reels/week, never a dark week); reach ~500
  engaged local followers; clay series live and recognisable.
- **6 months:** be the **most-followed / most-active** JHB tennis coaching account; free-lesson DMs/clicks
  as the tracked outcome (not follower count).
- **Always:** measure to the **bottom of the funnel** — free-lesson bookings, not likes (see `social-loop.md`).

> Bottom line: this is a winnable category. The competition is quiet, lookalike, and selling the same free
> lesson. We have the only clay court in Gauteng, genuine tour-level coaching, and a venue worth filming.
> Show up consistently with *place + people + clay* and NextPoint can own JHB tennis on social within months.
