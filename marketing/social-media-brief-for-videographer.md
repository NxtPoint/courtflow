---

## How to send it (easiest first)
Drop everything into **one shared link** — whatever's simplest for you:
- **Google Drive / Dropbox folder** (share link, "anyone with link can view/download"), **WeTransfer**, or
  **Frame.io**. Any of these is fine.
- Please **don't** send via WhatsApp — it crushes the quality and we lose the originals.
- Inside the link, two folders: **`/VIDEO`** and **`/PHOTOS`**. If clips are already named or grouped by
  type (serves, rallies, juniors, clay, etc.), even better — but raw is fine, we'll sort.

### File specs (so it's usable for Reels/Stories)
- **Video:** send the **highest-resolution originals** you have (4K or 1080p). Vertical **9:16** clips are
  gold for Reels/Stories; landscape 16:9 is still useful — send both, we'll crop. MP4/MOV.
- **Photos:** full-res JPEG/PNG straight off the camera. Don't downscale.
- **Don't pre-edit/brand them** unless you want to — we caption + cut for social. Raw originals give us the
  most to work with.

## PART A — What to send from your existing library
If you've already shot any of these, they're exactly what we need. Send whatever exists:

**Video you likely already have:**
- Players **rallying** (baseline, wide framing) — the "this looks like a great club" b-roll.
- **Serves** — any angle, especially slow-mo or side/behind.
- **Coaching in action** — a coach feeding balls, correcting a player, a lesson moment.
- **Junior sessions** — kids drilling/playing (wide or from behind is fine for consent).
- **Cardio Tennis / group / social** — anything high-energy, people moving and laughing.
- **The clay court** — *any* footage of it. Slides, play, even just the surface. **This is our #1 asset —
  send every second of clay you've got.**
- **General club b-roll** — wide shots of the 8 courts, the setting, golden-hour, gates/entrance, the vibe.

**Photos you likely already have:**
- Hero shots of the courts (especially the **clay court**) — golden hour if you have it.
- **Coach Ross + coaching action shots** — Coach Ross with players, clean shots (no other coaches by name).
- Junior squad / group photos.
- Detail shots — racquets, balls, net, clay texture, signage/branding.
- Any **action stills** of good-looking tennis moments.

> Even if it's "old" footage — send it. We can get 3–4 weeks of posts out of a back catalogue.

## PART B — Short list to film (only if you're shooting anyway)
Prioritised. If you can only grab a few, **do the ⭐ ones first** — those are the posts we most want to run
and are missing. Each is just **seconds** of footage; we cut and caption afterwards.

### ⭐ Highest priority (unique to NextPoint — nobody else has these)
1. ⭐ **Clay court reveal + a slide** — a walk-on shot and someone sliding on the clay. The "only clay court
   in Gauteng" hero moment. Vertical 9:16. Get a few takes.
2. ⭐ **Coach tip to camera #1 — forehand** — Coach Ross, 15–20 sec, talking straight to the phone:
   *"The #1 forehand mistake club players make…"* Then a quick demo. (We'll feed the exact line if useful.)
3. ⭐ **Coach tip to camera #2 — serve** — same setup, a quick serve-toss or grip tip.
4. ⭐ **Coaching shots** (stills) — Coach Ross working with players, for a "Coaching" post.

### High priority (the "great club" feed)
5. **Slow-mo serve** — side angle + behind. Cinematic.
6. **A rally** — two players, baseline, wide. Clean and good-looking.
7. **"A day at NextPoint" b-roll** — gates → courts → coffee → first hits. A handful of 2–4 sec clips we
   fast-cut into one reel.
8. **8-courts wide pan** — a walk or drone-ish pan showing the scale of the facility.

### Good to have (programmes + people)
9. **Cardio Tennis** — high-energy class montage, people moving + music-friendly.
10. **Junior session** — kids drilling (faces only with parent consent; otherwise wide/behind).
11. **Member soundbite** — one member, 10–15 sec candid: *"Why I train at NextPoint."*
12. **Golden-hour hero photos** of each court + the clay.

### Filming specs (quick rules)
- **Shoot vertical (9:16)** for anything that could be a Reel/Story — that's most of it. Phone is totally
  fine; a recent iPhone/Android shoots great social video.
- **Hold each shot ~5–10 seconds**, steady. More short clips > one long one.
- **Get coach-tip audio clean** — film somewhere not too windy/noisy, coach close to the phone.
- **Consent for juniors** — only show kids' faces with parent permission; otherwise wide/back angles.
- Aim to come away with **~15–20 clips + 20–30 photos**. That's a month-plus of content from one session.
